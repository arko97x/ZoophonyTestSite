Zoophony VR is a spatial audio and virtual reality work developed as a collaboration between Berlin-based composer, Sergey Khismatov and Bangalore-based visual-artist, Chaitanya Krishnan. This work is the result of an obsessive, decade-long project by the Russian born Khismatov to collect animal sounds and use them in a symphony. Chaitanya worked with Sergey Khismatov to interpret the zoophony score and transform it into a claustrophobic, ghostly and sometimes magical atmosphere. Zoophony VR deals with environmental concerns that inform the practices of both the artists. Chaitanya Krishnan was assisted by the graduate trans-disciplinary research students at the (ART)ScienceBLR. The work was also supported by the STORYLAB at the Srishti Manipal Institute of Art, Design and Technology, Bangalore.

This collaboration took place as part of Soundchains - a Virtual Artistic Exchange program hosted at TheISRO and supported by Goethe Institut/Max Mueller Bhavan, Bangalore. This Virtual artistic exchange is a response to the pandemic. With the shutting down of the international physical residency at the Indian Sonic Research Organisation, production and collaboration has now moved into new uncharted digital formats.

ZOOPHONY CAN BE EXPERIENCED IN 3 WAYS

IN ALL CASES, HEADPHONES ARE HIGHLY RECOMMENDED

On your computer move around with the WASD or Arrow Keys, and the Mouse to look around

On your mobile device, tap the fullscreen button and then move the device to look around

On your mobile device or PC, tap the VR button to enable VR mode and use a VR headset
